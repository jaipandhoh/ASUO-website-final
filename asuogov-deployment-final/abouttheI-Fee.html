About the Incidental Fee (I-Fee)

Understanding how your student fees support campus programs and services

The I-Fee is a mandatory student fee determined annually by the ASUO through an open, democratic process. The current budget is ~$9 million and funds student programs and services.

What is the I-Fee?

The Incidental Fee (I-Fee) is a mandatory fee charged to all University of Oregon students. It's designed to support student programs, services, and activities that enhance the campus experience.

How is it determined?

Each year, ASUO coordinates a democratic process where students have input into how the I-Fee budget is allocated. This ensures that student priorities and needs are reflected in funding decisions.

What does it fund?

The I-Fee supports a wide range of programs including:

  • Student organizations and clubs
  • Campus events and programming
  • Student services and resources
  • Facility improvements
  • Leadership development programs

By paying the I-Fee, you're investing in your own student experience and helping to build a vibrant campus community!
