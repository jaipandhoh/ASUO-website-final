ASUO Elections

Your voice matters in student government

Each spring, ASUO members vote electronically for student officers and weigh in on referenda. The student-led Elections Board oversees the process and eligibility.

Why Elections Matter

  • Sets leadership direction and priorities for the student body.
  • Provides campus-wide input on initiatives and referenda.
  • Ensures representation of diverse student voices.
  • Builds democratic engagement among the student community.

Who Can Vote

Every ASUO member is eligible to vote during the spring election window. This includes all enrolled students who pay the Incidental Fee.

What You're Voting On

  • Student Body President - Leads the executive branch and represents student interests
  • Student Body Vice President - Supports the president and oversees specific initiatives
  • Student Senators - Represent different schools and colleges within the university
  • Referenda - Important policy questions that affect the entire student body

Get Involved

Beyond voting, you can also:

  • Run for office and become a candidate
  • Volunteer with campaigns
  • Join the Elections Board
  • Help spread awareness about important issues

Your participation in ASUO elections helps shape the future of student life at the University of Oregon!
