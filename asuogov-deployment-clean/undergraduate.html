Resources for Undergraduate Students

Everything you need to know about ASUO services and opportunities for undergraduate students at the University of Oregon.

üéì Getting Involved as an Undergraduate

As an undergraduate student, you have numerous opportunities to get involved with ASUO and make a difference on campus. Here are the key ways to participate:

Student Government Participation

  • Run for Senate: Represent your college or constituency in the ASUO Senate
  • Join Committees: Serve on various ASUO committees that shape campus policy
  • Attend Senate Meetings: Public meetings are held weekly and open to all students
  • Vote in Elections: Participate in annual ASUO elections for President, VP, and Senate

Student Organizations

  • Join a Club: Over 300 student organizations receive ASUO funding
  • Start a New Organization: Apply for recognition and funding for new student groups
  • Attend Events: Participate in ASUO-sponsored campus events and activities

üí∞ Financial Resources

ASUO manages the Incidental Fee, which funds essential student services and programs:

What Your I-Fee Covers

  • Student Organizations: Funding for clubs, sports, and cultural groups
  • Campus Services: EMU operations, student media, and recreational facilities
  • Student Government: ASUO operations and student advocacy programs
  • Health and Wellness: Counseling services and wellness programs

Financial Aid and Support

  • Emergency Funding: Apply for emergency financial assistance through ASUO
  • Scholarships: Various ASUO-sponsored scholarship opportunities
  • Work-Study: ASUO offers work-study positions for eligible students

Academic Advocacy

Make the most of your undergraduate experience with ASUO-sponsored activities:

Major Events

  • Flock Party: Annual welcome event for new and returning students
  • Street Faire: Bi-annual festival featuring student vendors and organizations
  • Cultural Events: Celebrations of diversity and cultural heritage
  • Academic Events: Lectures, workshops, and academic competitions

  • Community Service: Volunteer opportunities and service learning projects
  • Leadership Development: Workshops and programs to develop leadership skills
